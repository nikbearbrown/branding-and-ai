## FIGURE 1 · The flood and the filter · Important · conceptual map
AI made competent content nearly free, so volume is no longer scarce — judgment is. Left: "The flood" — cheap, fluent, generic content pouring across channels (no specific claim, no audience, no verified truth). Right: "The filter" — what rises in value when content gets cheap: the decision of what's worth saying, verification that it's true, the judgment that it sounds like you. An arrow shows cheap production raising, not lowering, the value of editorial judgment.

- RED: the central "editorial judgment" filter — the scarce thing AI cannot supply, the chapter's whole thesis.

## FIGURE 2 · The PESO model · Critical · systems diagram

Arrows between all four show how each feeds the others; the power is in integration, not any single quadrant.

## FIGURE 4 · The campaign brief: six questions in sequence · Critical · process flowchart
The campaign brief answers six questions in sequence; each constrains the next. Objective (specific, measurable change) → Audience (named, specific person) → Message (one testable claim) → Channel mix (which PESO channels, why, how they connect) → Budget (work backward from spend) → Measurement (one primary KPI per objective, set before launch). A one-page brief is finished; a three-page brief hasn't made its decisions.

## FIGURE 5 · Reach vs. frequency by goal · Important · quantitative chart
Reach = distinct people who see the message once; frequency = times each person sees it. The optimal mix depends on the goal. Awareness goal → high reach, low frequency. Conversion goal → high frequency, low reach (a single impression rarely changes behavior; the old rule was ~7 exposures). The common error is optimizing for reach when you need frequency.
- Spatial: a 2x2-style axes plot, x = reach, y = frequency, with two labeled optimal regions — "awareness" in the high-reach/low-frequency corner, "conversion" in the high-frequency/low-reach corner; a diagonal trade-off line at fixed budget.
- RED: the "conversion: high frequency" region — the under-used optimization small brands miss, the thing the chapter wants the reader to look at.
- EXCLUSIONS: no PESO detail, no specific spend figures beyond illustrative, no campaign-brief sections.
