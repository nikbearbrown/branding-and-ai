## FIGURE 1 · Years to build, hours to lose: the United Airlines timeline · Critical · timeline

## FIGURE 2 · SCCT: matching response to crisis type · Critical · matrix / taxonomy
Coombs's Situational Crisis Communication Theory: the right strategy depends on how much responsibility the public assigns. Three rows — victim (external force, low attribution → deny / bolster), accidental (unintended harm, moderate attribution → diminish), preventable (knew or should have known, high attribution → rebuild / mortification). Columns: crisis type · example · public responsibility · appropriate strategy · strategy that backfires. The recurring error: applying a low-responsibility strategy to a high-responsibility crisis.
- Spatial: three-row × five-column matrix, crisis type as row header ordered by rising responsibility attribution (victim → accidental → preventable).

## FIGURE 4 · The crisis playbook: four components · Important · hierarchy / structural diagram
The playbook is written before the fire. Four components, each with what it contains, who owns it, and when it is used: monitoring & alert thresholds (what you watch; noise vs. signal), severity triage (first question — which SCCT type is this?), holding statements (pre-drafted per crisis category), escalation path (who decides, who speaks, who approves, is legal review required). The slow decisions are made in advance so the fast moment is execution, not judgment.
- Spatial: four stacked component bands (monitoring → triage → holding statements → escalation), each annotated with contents / owner / when-used; an arrow down the side showing the pre-crisis → live-moment flow.

## FIGURE 5 · Speed vs. wrong-speed: the holding statement resolves the tension · Important · comparison / decision diagram
The advice "respond fast" is incomplete — fast with the wrong message is worse than silence. Three outcomes contrasted: silence (cedes the narrative; observers fill the vacuum), wrong-fast (a defensive or minimizing statement issued before the facts, falsified when facts emerge), and the holding statement (fast and commits to nothing unsustainable — "we are investigating," "customer safety is our priority"). The holding statement sits at the intersection of fast and accurate, buying time to get the real response right.
- Spatial: three columns or a small 2-axis layout (speed vs. accuracy) placing silence, wrong-fast, and holding statement; the holding statement marked as the resolution of the tension; propagation-based posting thresholds noted (social ≤2h, trade ≈1 day, broadcast pre-queued).
