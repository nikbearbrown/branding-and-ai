## FIGURE 1 · Standardize, adapt, or glocalize · Critical · comparison panels
The 1983 Levitt question resolved at neither pole. Three columns laid against the same brand: standardize (one identity everywhere — efficiency, global recognition, lower cost), adapt (change expression per market — local resonance at higher coordination cost), glocalize (global core, local execution — the synthesis most brands land on). Each column carries its buy and its cost so the trade-off reads as real and undissolved.
- Spatial: three vertical panels divided by hairlines; row labels down the left (strategy, what it buys, what it costs, example posture); McDonald's glocalization as the worked column.
- RED: none — three peer strategic options compared neutrally; no single focal mark.
- EXCLUSIONS: no per-element split table (Fig 2), no Hofstede content, no transcreation rings.

## FIGURE 2 · The keep-global / localize split, element by element · Critical · matrix
Glocalization is decided per element, not once for the whole brand. Rows of brand elements — brand name, visual identity, tagline, product formulation, channel mix, pricing, imagery, humor — each marked as typically holds global or typically localizes, with the deciding question that settles it. Makes the abstract core/expression rule concrete and operational.
- Spatial: 8-row × 3-column matrix (element · typically holds global / typically localizes · deciding question); a center column of marks showing the global/local lean per row.
- RED: none — a reference taxonomy of per-element decisions; no focal element.
- EXCLUSIONS: no strategy comparison (Fig 1), no Hofstede dimensions (Fig 3), no concentric core/expression rings (Fig 5).

## FIGURE 3 · Hofstede's dimensions as a brand-message lens · Important · matrix
Hofstede's dimensions explain why one message reads differently across cultures — used to ask better questions, never to script individuals. Rows: power distance, individualism/collectivism, uncertainty avoidance, masculinity/femininity, long-term orientation, indulgence/restraint. Columns: high-score tendency, low-score tendency, brand-message implication. A standing caveat band: population tendency, not individual script.
- Spatial: 6-row × 3-column matrix with a full-width caveat strip pinned at the bottom; individualism/collectivism row visually weighted as the most message-relevant.
- RED: none — a neutral reference framework; the caveat does the warning work in type, not color.
- EXCLUSIONS: no standardize/adapt strategy (Fig 1), no per-element split (Fig 2), no transcreation rings.

## FIGURE 4 · Three levels of language adaptation · Critical · structural schematic (nested rings)
Three escalating levels of effort. Translation (outer ring — words; preserves literal meaning, insufficient alone). Localization (middle ring — words + context; formats, units, references; the standard for functional content). Transcreation (inner ring — words + context + emotional intent; re-creates the feeling, may share no words; where brand work happens). Each ring annotated with what it preserves and what it adds.
- Spatial: three concentric rings, widest outer = translation, innermost = transcreation; leader labels naming what each layer preserves; the inner core labelled "produces the same feeling."
- RED: the innermost transcreation core — the single level the chapter says is where brand work actually happens and the one students skip. One focal mark, named.
- EXCLUSIONS: no Hofstede dimensions, no blunder checklist, no market-entry workflow.

## FIGURE 5 · Core holds, expression flexes · Important · structural schematic (concentric)
The rule that anchors glocalization: the core stays global, the expression localizes. Concentric zones — innermost (purpose / archetype / fundamental promise — global, fixed), middle (visual identity, voice principles — mostly global, light adaptation), outer (language, imagery, references, channel, seasonal — adapts per market). Two failure modes annotated at the margins: too rigid (holding expression fixed — tone-deaf) and too loose (flexing the core — fragmentation).
- Spatial: three concentric zones from fixed center outward to fully-adapting rim; "too rigid" and "too loose" failure callouts on opposite sides via leader lines.
- RED: none — a structural reference of what is fixed vs. flexible; the two failure callouts are peers, neither is the single focal mark.
- EXCLUSIONS: no three-level language rings (Fig 4), no Hofstede matrix, no testing checklist.

## FIGURE 6 · Cross-cultural testing checklist · Supplementary · matrix
The lesson survives whether any blunder anecdote is true: test with embedded native speakers, not dictionaries. Rows of elements to test — name, tagline, colors, imagery, humor, numerical/symbolic associations. Columns: what to test for, who to involve, what a red flag looks like, verification standard. Apocryphal anecdotes (Nova, Coors) noted as not-required to make the case.
- Spatial: 6-row × 4-column checklist matrix; a footer note flagging that several famous blunder stories are myths.
- RED: none — a procedural reference checklist; no focal row.
- EXCLUSIONS: no language-adaptation rings, no Hofstede matrix, no AI+1 workflow.

## FIGURE 7 · The three-stage market-entry plan, with the AI+1 boundary · Critical · process flowchart
The worked example as a workflow with a clear human line. Stage 1: keep-global / localize split (AI + Hofstede lens → rationale per element). Stage 2: transcreation (AI drafts → native speaker verifies). Stage 3: risk audit (AI flags name/color/imagery/number risks → native verifies each flag). A dashed AI+1 boundary runs through every stage: AI generates and flags; cultural judgment — lives in communities, not models — verifies.
- Spatial: left-to-right three-stage flow; each stage split top (AI drafts/flags) and bottom (native verifies) by a dashed horizontal AI+1 boundary line spanning the figure.
- RED: the AI+1 verification boundary — the single non-delegable line the chapter calls the clearest since the trademark chapter; the thing the reader must see first.
- EXCLUSIONS: no Hofstede dimension detail, no testing-checklist rows, no core/expression rings.
