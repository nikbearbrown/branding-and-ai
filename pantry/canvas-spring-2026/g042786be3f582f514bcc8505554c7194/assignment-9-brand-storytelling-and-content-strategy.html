Making Your Brand Unforgettable

Due: April 3, 2026 at 11:59 PM | Total Points: 100 Storytelling makes them remember you. This assignment is about developing the narrative layer of your brand — the stories you'll use in your About page, LinkedIn summary, pitch conversations, and presentations. By the end, you'll have three battle-ready mini-stories and one expanded narrative ready to publish.

The best brand stories aren't about you. They're about a transformation — a problem encountered, a decision made, a change that happened — and the person reading recognizes something of themselves in it.

Continue with your chosen path:

  • Path A: Personal brand storytelling
  • Path B: Madison AI tool startup storytelling

Part 1: Develop Your Brand Storytelling (60 Points)

Three Brand Story Arcs (35 Points)

Write three 100-word brand stories, each using a different narrative framework. These aren't writing exercises — they're deployable brand assets. Each one should work as a standalone piece on your website, LinkedIn About section, a conference bio, or a social post.

Choose 3 frameworks from:

Framework

Core Movement

The Hero's Journey

Challenge encountered → transformation → return with solution

The Quest

Mission identified → obstacles faced → goal achieved

Overcoming the Monster

Threat identified → confrontation → victory → new order

Rags to Riches

Humble beginning → opportunity seized → setback → growth → success

The Mentor

Need recognized → guidance offered → student transformed → legacy

Path A — Personal Brand Story Angles:

  • A pivotal career moment that changed your professional direction
  • How you developed your signature approach or methodology
  • A failure or obstacle that shaped who you are professionally
  • Your "why" — what drives the work beyond the paycheck
  • How your Madison tool changed the way you work

Path B — Madison Tool Startup Story Angles:

  • The origin story: why you built this tool, what need it answered
  • The problem your tool solves, told through a specific user's pain
  • Your tool's mission — the world it's trying to create
  • A specific transformation your tool enables
  • Your competitive insight: what you saw that others missed

Each 100-word story must:

  • Use one clear narrative framework (name it at the top)
  • Include specific, concrete details — no generic statements
  • Have a clear beginning, middle, and end
  • Sound like a human being, not a LinkedIn post generator
  • Be ready to publish as-is

Expanded Brand Story (25 Points)

Select your strongest arc and expand it into a 300-word version using a five-point structure:

  1. Setup: Establish the scene, challenge, or opportunity
  2. Rising Action: What happened, what you discovered, what was at stake
  3. Turning Point: The key moment, decision, or insight that changed everything
  4. Resolution: What changed, what you created, what you achieved
  5. Takeaway: What this means for your audience, clients, or users

This is your About page story. It should feel earned, specific, and true. Write it in your own voice — not the voice you think a brand should have.

Part 2: Content Strategy & Publication (40 Points)

LinkedIn, Substack Article or Blog Post (40 Points)

Write and publish a 500-word article that uses your strongest storytelling arc as its backbone. This piece goes on LinkedIn Articles, Medium, or your personal website/blog — not just in a PDF submitted to Canvas.

Path A — Personal Brand Article:

  • Lead with your expertise and unique professional point of view
  • Weave in your Madison tool as a natural extension of your capabilities
  • Use your story arc to give the piece narrative structure
  • Position yourself as a practitioner worth following

Path B — Madison Tool Startup Article:

  • Tell your tool's story in a way that makes the reader care about the problem
  • Use your story arc to build toward your value proposition
  • Show transformation — before and after using your tool
  • End with a clear call-to-action

Requirements:

  • 500 words minimum
  • Your chosen narrative framework is visible in the structure
  • At least one custom graphic: tool screenshot, branded image, process diagram, or before/after visual
  • Published live (include the URL)
  • Professional formatting: headline, subheadings, short paragraphs, scannable
  • Promoted on LinkedIn with a 2–3 sentence caption that earns the click

On Using AI to Develop Your Stories

You can and should use Claude (claude.ai) to help you structure and refine your storytelling. Bring your raw material — bullet points about your journey, key moments, outcomes — and use prompts like:

"Help me structure these bullet points into a Hero's Journey narrative arc. I need it to be about 100 words and sound like a real person, not a press release."

"I'm writing a brand story about my Madison tool. Here are my key points: [paste bullets]. Can you help me build a five-point Quest arc around them?"

Use AI to sharpen and structure. The specific details, the authentic voice, and the decision about what's actually true and meaningful — that's yours.
