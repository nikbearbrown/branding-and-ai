Self-Promotion Through Thought Leadership

Due: 4/10/2026 at 11:59 PM | Total Points: 100 (80 core + 20 quality)

Overview

You have a Madison AI tool. You have a brand. Now you need to be findable, credible, and worth following. This assignment is about learning to use writing as a strategic brand asset — not a homework exercise.

Substack has become one of the most powerful platforms for professionals and founders to build audiences, establish authority, and create content that compounds over time. Unlike social media posts that disappear in 48 hours, a well-written Substack article lives, gets shared, gets indexed, and builds your professional reputation while you sleep.

In Assignment 9 you published a LinkedIn article — a single piece of content on a borrowed platform. This assignment is different. You're building a home. A place you own, with a subscriber list you control, where your brand voice develops over time. The Substack article you write here draws directly on the story arcs and expanded narrative you developed in Assignment 9 — this is where that storytelling work gets deployed.

Part 1: Launch Your Substack Publication (20 Points)

Publication Setup (10 Points)

Create and configure your Substack publication with full brand alignment:

  • Publication name: Reflects your personal brand or Madison tool brand
  • Tagline: One sharp sentence that tells readers exactly what they'll get
  • About page: 150–200 words introducing you and the publication's focus
  • Logo or header image: Uses your established visual brand assets from Assignments 7–8
  • Custom URL: Set up your Substack subdomain (yourname.substack.com)

Your Substack should look like it belongs to the same brand family as your website and LinkedIn. Not identical — but clearly related.

Deliverable: Live Substack publication link

Brand Consistency Audit (10 Points)

Submit a one-page PDF documenting your brand consistency choices across your Substack and your existing digital presence:

  • Screenshot comparison: Substack header / LinkedIn header / Website hero section
  • Brief written explanation (3–5 sentences) of how the three platforms feel cohesive

Deliverable: Brand consistency audit PDF

Part 2: Write and Publish Your First Article (40 Points)

The Article (30 Points)

Write and publish a 600–800 word thought leadership article that does three things simultaneously:

  1. Establishes you as a credible voice in your domain
  2. Introduces your Madison AI tool and demonstrates its value
  3. Gives the reader something genuinely useful — a framework, an insight, a perspective they didn't have before

This is not a product announcement. This is a demonstration of your thinking. The best self-promotion never feels like self-promotion.

Choose your angle:

Path A — Personal Brand:

  • Your unique perspective on a problem in your industry
  • A lesson learned from your professional journey that others can apply
  • Why the conventional approach to [your area of expertise] is broken — and what works instead
  • How AI tools (including your Madison tool) are changing the nature of your work

Path B — Madison Tool Startup:

  • The industry problem your tool was built to solve — told through a story
  • A counterintuitive truth about branding, AI, or your target market
  • A framework or methodology your tool is built on, explained for practitioners
  • What you learned from building your tool that surprised you

Article Requirements:

  • 600–800 words
  • Published live on Substack (not a draft)
  • Compelling headline that earns the click
  • Opening hook: the first two sentences must earn the third
  • At least one custom visual: tool screenshot, branded graphic, process diagram, or data visualization created in Canva, Figma, or Adobe Express
  • Your Madison tool is referenced naturally within the article — not forced, not buried
  • Professional formatting: subheadings, short paragraphs, scannable structure
  • Ends with a clear call-to-action: subscribe, try the tool, connect on LinkedIn, comment with a response

Deliverable: Live Substack article link

Promotion Post (10 Points)

After publishing, promote your article with a post on LinkedIn. Your post should:

  • Include a 2–4 sentence caption that gives value or teases the key insight — not just "I wrote a thing, check it out"
  • Link to your Substack article
  • Include a screenshot or graphic from your article as the post image

Deliverable: Screenshot of your LinkedIn post (with visible engagement metrics if any)

Part 3: Reflection (20 Points)

Write a 300-word reflection (submitted in your PDF) addressing:

  • What strategic choices did you make in your article — angle, tone, structure — and why?
  • How does this article position you differently from others in your field?
  • What would you do differently if you wrote it again?
  • If you were to publish this article on a platform you owned — a newsletter, a blog — what would the next piece be about? (One sentence is enough.)

This reflection is how we know you were thinking strategically, not just executing mechanically.

Deliverable: Reflection section in submission PDF

What Makes This Hard (And Why That's the Point)

Writing for an audience — even a small one — is different from writing for a professor. You have to earn attention you were never promised. That's the skill. Published and imperfect beats perfect and invisible. Ship it.
