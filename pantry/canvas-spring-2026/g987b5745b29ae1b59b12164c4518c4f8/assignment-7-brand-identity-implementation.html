Building the Visual Foundation of Your Brand

Due: March 20, 2026 at midnight | Submission: PDF + Figma board link | Total Points: 100 (80 core + 20 quality)

Overview

This is where your brand strategy becomes something you can see, feel, and touch. Building on Assignment 6 (your personal brand or Madison tool startup), you'll create the three tangible pillars of brand identity: a creative brief that locks in your strategy, a visual identity system that expresses your brand personality, and website wireframes that map the user's journey. Apply the 80/20 principle — focus on the work that moves the needle, not perfection.

Choose your path (same path you chose in Assignment 6):

  • Path A: Personal professional brand
  • Path B: Your Madison AI tool as a startup brand

Component 1: Creative Brief (20 Points)

A creative brief is the strategic backbone of everything visual that follows. Think of it as a technical requirements document for creative elements. Write yours using the structure below. Be specific — vague briefs produce vague design.

Objective

State your brand's primary goal in 1–2 sentences.

Path A Example: Position myself as the go-to UX designer for healthcare technology companies that need someone who understands both clinical workflows and modern interface design.

Path B Example: Launch MadisonLens as the AI branding tool that helps early-stage startups define their brand archetype and messaging in under 10 minutes.

Target Audience

Identify 2–3 key audience segments with brief descriptions. Know who you're talking to. List as primary, secondary, and tertiary audiences.

Path A Example:

  • Healthcare tech hiring managers who are seeking specialized UX expertise
  • Clinical staff who evaluate and champion software procurement
  • Industry publications looking for thought leadership on healthcare UX & recruiters

Path B Example:

  • VCs and investors
  • Accelerator programs looking for AI-powered branding workshops
  • General public awareness and PR

Key Message (Your UVP)

One clear sentence. The thing only you can say. Why I win.

Path A: I turn the complexity of healthcare workflows into digital interfaces medical professionals can learn in minutes, not months.

Path B: MadisonLens cuts brand strategy from weeks to minutes by using AI to decode your archetype, sharpen your message, and generate your visual direction — all in one session.

Support Points

3–5 points that show how you deliver on your UVP. Be specific — give concrete examples for each.

Tone & Style

How you sound and how you look — in a few words. (E.g., "Bold and data-driven, approachable in social, precise in professional contexts.") This is where your archetype comes in.

Competitive Positioning

What makes you different from the alternatives? Be honest and specific.

Success Metrics

How will you know this brand is working? Career outcomes, engagement numbers, contracts secured — make it measurable.

Component 2: Visual Identity Development (30 Points)

Logo Development

Create 4–5 logo concepts using Figma, Adobe Express, or Canva AI (https://canva.com/ai).

  • Explore different approaches: wordmark, icon, combination mark
  • Document your iteration process — show your thinking
  • Ensure it works across all media — or create variations: one optimized for a mobile header, another that holds up at billboard scale
  • Write a clear rationale for your final selection: why this one, not the others

Mood Board

Assemble the visual world of your brand:

  • Color System: Use Coolors (https://coolors.co) to develop 2–3 primary and 2–3 secondary colors. Include hex codes and brief rationale for each color choice.
  • Typography: Heading font, body font, optional accent font — with written rationale for each selection. Keep it to one or two fonts.
  • Visual Style: Photography direction, illustration style, iconography guidelines
  • Inspiration Grid: 8–10 curated images that capture your brand's essence, each with a one-sentence explanation of why it belongs

Brand Style Guide (Start on Figma)

Begin building the document your future designer-self will thank you for:

  • Logo usage rules: sizing, clear space, placement
  • Color application guidelines: what goes where and why

Component 3: Website Wireframes (30 Points)

Use Figma wireframe kits (https://figma.com/templates/wireframe-kits/) to build low-fidelity wireframes for the following five pages. Focus on structure and user flow, not visual design.

Required Pages

  1. Homepage
  • Hero section with your UVP prominently placed
  • Navigation structure
  • Featured work, projects, or product offerings
  • Primary call-to-action
  1. About
  • Your brand narrative, connected to your archetype
  • Mission, vision, values
  • Personal journey or company origin story
  • What makes you/it different
  1. Portfolio/Projects OR Products/Services
  • Work sample or product layout structure
  • Case study or feature framework
  • Categorization logic
  1. Resume/Experience OR Team/Expertise
  • Professional or company timeline
  • Skills, expertise, credentials
  • Key achievements and milestones
  1. Contact
  • Form design
  • Social media integration
  • Clear call-to-action messaging

Additional Requirements

  • Mobile-responsive design for the homepage (at minimum)
  • User flow documentation: how do people move through the site?
  • Interactive element planning: what should respond to user actions?
  • Brief platform comparison: Vercel v0, Framer AI, or Wix — which will you build on and why?

Common Pitfalls to Avoid

  • Generic visual identity that could belong to any brand in any industry
  • Creative brief that doesn't connect to the design choices that follow
  • Wireframes that skip user experience thinking entirely
  • Mood board assembled without rationale — just pretty pictures
  • Rushing past visual identity to get to wireframes
