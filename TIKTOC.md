# Branding and AI — BLUEPRINT
## Textbook Architecture (Vision → Architecture → Chapter Spec → Risk)

**Book type:** Graduate branding course + practitioner handbook (AI+1 series)
**Status:** Re-architecture. The prior 12-chapter manuscript is a *subset* of this plan
(the "build-and-brand-one-tool" spine). This blueprint expands it into a **complete
branding curriculum done hands-on with AI**.
**Subtitle:** *AI+1, Hands on. Domain specific. No generic prompts.*

> **Why the rewrite.** The old book taught a narrow slice — build an AI tool, brand it —
> and skipped most of the branding canon (brand equity, audience/segmentation, brand
> architecture, measurement, management/rebranding, experience/touchpoints, ethics &
> sustainability, IP/trademark, global, crisis, media). This blueprint teaches
> **everything a standard branding book teaches**, and teaches each topic the AI+1 way:
> the modern brander does not just *know* branding — they *run the tools*. Every canon
> topic is paired with doing it hands-on through **Madison** (the bundled framework),
> driven from **Cowork / Codex / Claude Code**.

---

## Vision

**The one reader.** A technically capable person — engineer, founder, analyst, designer-who-codes —
who must now own brand work and will do it with AI agents, not a creative agency.

**Central argument.** When AI collapses the cost of *producing* brand artifacts, the scarce
skills become brand *judgment* (what to build, for whom, what to refuse) and *operating the
tools* that execute. The modern brander is a **Creative Engineer**: they run Cowork/Codex and
frameworks like Madison to do, at speed, the full job a branding department used to do — while
keeping the accountable decisions human (AI+1).

**What the reader can do at the end.** Run the entire branding lifecycle — audience research,
positioning, architecture, identity, naming/IP, voice, story, experience, content/campaigns,
measurement, governance, ethics review, crisis response, global adaptation, and launch — as
AI-operated workflows they direct and verify.

**Comparable texts & the gap.**
- *The Fundamentals of Branding* (Davis) and Keller/Aaker on brand equity — teach the canon, **no AI execution**.
- *Building a StoryBrand*, *Designing Brand Identity* (Wheeler) — strong on one slice, no tooling.
- AI-marketing trade books — tool tours with **no branding rigor**.
- **This book** is the only one that teaches the full canon *and* makes the reader operate the AI that does it.

---

## The spine (four theses, every chapter)

1. **Creative Engineer.** The brander runs the tools; credentials cheapened, judgment + operation didn't.
2. **AI+1.** The agent drafts; the human owns the decision (archetype, positioning, claim, sign-off).
3. **Learn Cowork/Codex through Madison.** CLI-agent skill is taught by *doing branding work* with the bundled framework, not in the abstract.
4. **Canon, done hands-on.** Each standard-branding topic is taught, then executed as a Madison/AI workflow with a verification gate.

## Architecture — six parts, three-act arc

**Act I — Establish** (Parts I–II): what a brand is, the tools, who it's for, what it stands for.
**Act II — Build** (Parts III–IV): make the brand legible; build the product that carries it.
**Act III — Apply** (Parts V–VI): run, measure, govern, defend, and launch the brand — and the brander.

## Chapter anatomy (every chapter)

Cold-open brand decision → canon taught from first principles → **Cowork/Codex how-to** (operate Madison for this task) → worked example on the running brand → **AI+1 five-part block** (When to Use AI / When NOT / Recipe / CLI / Validation) → TL;DR + section previews (top) → graduated exercises → AI Wayback Machine → key terms → bridge.

## Running project

**Self-as-Project — *your brand, end to end*** (personal 8a / startup 8b fork at Part II),
advanced one artifact per chapter by running the mapped Madison recipe.

`key-terms`, Canvas) — desired, generated after the spine.

## Risk

- **Scope.** 23 chapters is a big book; biggest adoption risk is finishing. *Mitigation:* the 12 HAVE chapters already ship; the 10 NEW chapters are independent and can land in priority order. **Highest-value NEW first:** Ch2 (equity), Ch19 (ethics/sustainability — the canon emphasis the book most lacks), Ch17 (measurement), Ch4 (audience).
- **Tool dating.** Cowork/Codex/Madison specifics age. *Mitigation:* teach the decision; treat tool steps as swappable; keep Madison vendored + versioned.
- **Two books in one.** Canon + build-a-tool could feel like two books. *Mitigation:* the Creative-Engineer spine + Self-as-Project running brand unify them; Part IV (build) is explicitly the product that *carries* the brand from Parts II–III.
- **Comparable texts** cover canon *or* tooling, never both — the differentiator, but reviewers may expect one or the other; the intro must set the frame.
