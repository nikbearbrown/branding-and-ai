## Why this chapter exists
The book's own source text (*Fundamentals of Branding*) hammers ethics (~75 mentions) and
sustainability (~30); the book had essentially none. This is the single most important canon gap,
and it's where AI+1's "verify every claim" discipline matters most.

## Canon & sources
- **Brand activism / purpose:** Douglas Holt on cultural brands & activism; Simon Sinek's "Start
  With Why" (teach it *and* its critiques — purpose without proof is liability). Edelman Trust
  Barometer for the trust context. `[verify]` latest Edelman edition/figures.
- **CSR / ESG / B Corp:** corporate social responsibility, ESG reporting, B Corp certification
  (B Lab) as a purpose signal. `[verify]` current B Corp standards (B Lab announced revised
  standards) and ESG-reporting regime status.
- **Greenwashing & regulation (current — verified June 2026):**
  - **EU:** the standalone **Green Claims Directive (GCD)** proposal was **withdrawn (June 2025)**
    and is *not* expected to enter force. The binding regime is the **Empowering Consumers for the
    Green Transition (ECGT) Directive** (adopted March 2024): member-state transposition by **27
    March 2026**, rules apply from **27 September 2026** — bans generic green claims and
    **offset-based "carbon neutral" product claims**.
  - **US:** the **FTC Green Guides** (last revised 2012) have been under review since 2022; as of
    early 2026 **no updated version released**, expected 2026. State-level recyclable/compostable
    labeling rules are moving faster.
- **Woke-washing / purpose-washing** critique — performative values without behavior change erode
  trust faster than silence.

## Core concepts to teach
Purpose as commitment-under-cost (the values test from Ch 6, applied to ethics); the claim→proof
discipline; greenwashing as a *brand* risk and now a *legal* one; substantiation requirements;
DEI/social-impact branding and its backlash dynamics; why AI-generated purpose copy is dangerous
(fluent, unfalsifiable claims).

## Common misconceptions
- Purpose = a mission statement (it's behavior under cost, or it's washing).
- "Carbon neutral via offsets" is a safe claim (EU bans it for products from Sept 2026).
- Sustainability claims are marketing's call (they're now substantiation/legal calls).
