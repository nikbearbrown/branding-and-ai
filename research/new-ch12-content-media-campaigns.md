# Research — Ch 12: Content, Media & Campaigns

**Slot:** Core, Part IV · **Status:** NEW · **Madison:** `madison-campaign-construction`, `madison-copy-content-generation`, `content-agent`
**Running-project deliverable:** a campaign plan + a channel-ready content set for the chosen brand.

## Why this chapter exists
Content was handled only through Madison copy recipes with no media/campaign strategy. This chapter
adds the planning layer: what to say, where, why, and how it's paid for.

## Canon & sources
- **PESO model:** Paid, Earned, Shared, Owned media (Gini Dietrich, *Spin Sucks*) — the modern
  channel taxonomy; integrate rather than silo.
- **Integrated Marketing Communications (IMC):** Don Schultz — one voice across all channels; the
  consistency discipline at the campaign level.
- **Campaign planning:** objective → audience (Ch 4) → message (from positioning, Ch 6) → channel
  mix → budget → measurement (Ch 13). The brief drives the campaign.
- **Media mix / flighting**, reach vs. frequency; content strategy (editorial calendar, pillars).
- AI's role: drafting variants at volume (the cheapened-production thesis) — and why human taste
  and brand-fit gate what ships.

## Core concepts to teach
PESO as an integrated system; the campaign brief; message-channel fit; reach/frequency basics; an
editorial calendar with provenance (every claim sourced — ties to Ch 15 ethics).

## Common misconceptions
- Content = social posts (it's the full PESO system).
- More content = more impact (consistency and fit beat volume).
- AI-drafted copy can ship unedited (voice/authenticity/claims gate it — the AI+1 line).

## AI+1 / Madison hands-on

set. Human owns: the message, the brand voice sign-off, and every factual claim. Validation: strip
fabricated stats/quotes; check each variant against the archetype.
