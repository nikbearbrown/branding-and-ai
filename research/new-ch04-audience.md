# Research — Ch 4: The Brand Audience

**Slot:** Core, Part II · **Status:** NEW · **Madison:** `madison-audience-definition`, `madison-persona-generation`, `survey-analysis`
**Running-project deliverable:** an evidence-backed audience definition + 2 personas for the chosen brand.

## Why this chapter exists
Personas were generated ad hoc via Madison with no grounding in audience theory. This chapter
installs the discipline: who the brand is *for*, and how to know rather than guess.

## Canon & sources
- **STP — Segmentation, Targeting, Positioning:** Kotler & Keller, *Marketing Management*. The
  spine of audience strategy; positioning (Ch 6) is downstream of it.
- **Jobs-to-be-Done:** Clayton Christensen — people "hire" a brand for a job; segment by job, not
  demographic. Source: *Competing Against Luck*.
- **Cultural branding:** Douglas Holt, *How Brands Become Icons* — brands win by addressing
  cultural tensions, not just functional needs.
- **Psychographics:** VALS framework (SRI/Strategic Business Insights); segmentation by values &
  lifestyle, not just age/income.
- Bases of segmentation: demographic, geographic, behavioral, psychographic, needs/JTBD.

## Core concepts to teach
Segment → target → position as a sequence; the difference between a *segment* (a group with shared
needs) and a *demographic* (a descriptor); a persona as a research artifact, not a creative-writing
exercise; the cultural/JTBD lens for finding an under-served segment.

## Common misconceptions
- Demographics = segments (age 25–34 is not a need).
- "Everyone is our audience" (the positioning-killer).
- AI personas treated as real without evidence — the central AI+1 failure here.
