# Research — Ch 14: Brand Management, Governance & Rebranding

**Slot:** Core, Part IV · **Status:** NEW · **Madison:** `madison-brand-consistency-contradiction-checker`
**Running-project deliverable:** brand guidelines (a living system) + a rebrand-or-refresh decision memo.

## Why this chapter exists
The book taught how to *create* a brand, not how to *keep* one coherent over time and at scale, or
when/how to change it — the day-to-day of real brand work.

## Canon & sources
- **Brand guidelines as a living system & brand stewardship:** Alina Wheeler, *Designing Brand
  Identity* — governance, asset management, consistency at scale.
- **DAM (Digital Asset Management)** & design systems — how consistency is enforced operationally.
- **Rebranding triggers & risk:** the same cautionary cases as archetype drift (Tropicana 2009,
  Gap 2010, New Coke 1985) reframed as governance failures; **refresh vs. rebrand** (evolution vs.
  revolution); migration/transition management.
- **Brand consistency** as the compounding mechanism (ties to Ch 2 equity and the running-project
  "consistency is the brand" thread).

## Core concepts to teach
Guidelines as rules that must live and be enforced (not a PDF on a shelf); stewardship roles;
consistency at scale; the rebrand decision (legitimate triggers — M&A, repositioning, damage — vs.
vanity); refresh vs. full rebrand; migration risk (don't strand equity).

## Common misconceptions
- A brand book = brand governance (it's the artifact, not the practice).
- Rebrand = new logo (it's strategy; the logo is the visible 5%).
- Consistency = rigidity (a system flexes within rules — distinguish intentional variation from drift).
