# Research — Ch 13: Measuring Brand Equity & Impact

**Slot:** Core, Part IV · **Status:** NEW · **Madison:** `madison-performance-reporting`, `madison-category-sentiment-dashboard`
**Running-project deliverable:** a brand measurement plan (metric | baseline | target | source) for the chosen brand.

## Why this chapter exists
Davis's *Fundamentals* dedicates a whole chapter to "Assessing brand impact"; the book had none.
This closes it — and pairs with Ch 2 (equity is the asset; this is how you track it).

## Canon & sources
- **Brand tracking studies:** aided/unaided awareness, consideration, preference, usage — the
  longitudinal brand-health panel.
- **NPS (Net Promoter Score):** Fred Reichheld (*The Ultimate Question*) — the "would you
  recommend" metric; teach its uses *and* its well-documented limitations (single-item, cultural
  skew, not a strategy).
- **Keller CBBE measurement** (brand audits, brand value chain) — ties tracking back to the Ch 2
  pyramid.
- **Marketing metrics & ROI:** Farris et al., *Marketing Metrics*; marketing-mix modeling (MMM)
  and multi-touch attribution; brand-lift studies.
- Distinguish **brand metrics** (awareness, equity, sentiment) from **performance metrics**
  (conversion, CAC, ROAS) — both matter, they answer different questions.

## Core concepts to teach
What to measure and why; leading vs. lagging brand indicators; the limits of any single metric
(NPS especially); baselines and targets; attribution's hard problem (correlation ≠ causal lift —
echoes the AI+1 "when NOT to trust the dashboard" judgment).

## Common misconceptions
- NPS = brand health (it's one lagging proxy).
- A metric moving = the brand working (attribution/causality problem).
- Vanity metrics (impressions, followers) = equity.
