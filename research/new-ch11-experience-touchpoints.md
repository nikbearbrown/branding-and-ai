# Research — Ch 11: Brand Experience & Touchpoints

**Slot:** Core, Part III · **Status:** NEW · **Madison:** `ai-concierge`
**Running-project deliverable:** a customer-journey map + touchpoint audit for the chosen brand.

## Why this chapter exists
The old book's only "experience" was one tool's interface. Brands live across many touchpoints; the
experience *is* the brand for most customers.

## Canon & sources
- **Experience Economy:** Pine & Gilmore — goods → services → *experiences* as the value frontier;
  staging experiences as the brand differentiator.
- **Customer journey mapping** & **service blueprinting** (Shostack) — front-stage/back-stage,
  mapping the end-to-end path and the moments that carry the brand.
- **Moments of truth:** P&G's First/Second Moment of Truth; Google's **ZMOT** (Zero Moment of
  Truth) — the pre-purchase research moment.
- **Omnichannel** consistency; touchpoint inventory (owned/earned/paid surfaces a customer hits).
- CX measurement links forward to Ch 13 (CSAT/NPS at touchpoints).

## Core concepts to teach
The journey as the unit of brand experience (not the single ad or screen); touchpoint audit; where
the archetype/voice must hold across surfaces; the highest-leverage moments of truth; consistency
vs. channel-appropriate adaptation.

## Common misconceptions
- Experience = UI/UX of the product (it's every contact, pre- to post-purchase).
- More touchpoints = better (coherence beats coverage).
- Experience is the design team's job (it's a brand-strategy job).
