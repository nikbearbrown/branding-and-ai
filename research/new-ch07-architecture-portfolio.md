# Research — Ch 7: Brand Architecture & Portfolio

**Slot:** Core, Part II · **Status:** NEW · **Madison:** `madison-brand-portfolio-dashboard`
**Running-project deliverable:** a brand-architecture decision + portfolio map for the chosen brand (even a personal brand has sub-brands: projects, talks, products).

## Why this chapter exists
The book was single-brand. Real branding manages *systems* of brands — the structural choice that
governs naming, endorsement, and how equity flows between offerings.

## Canon & sources
- **Brand Relationship Spectrum:** Aaker & Joachimsthaler, *Brand Leadership* — the canonical
  model from **Branded House** (one master brand, e.g. Google, Apple, Virgin) ↔ **House of Brands**
  (independent brands, e.g. P&G, Unilever), with **Sub-brands** (Apple → iPhone) and **Endorsed
  brands** (Marriott → Courtyard by Marriott) in between.
- **Kapferer** on brand architecture & extension strategy (*The New Strategic Brand Management*).
- **Brand extension** & **co-branding/licensing**: line vs. category extension; extension risk
  (dilution, the Davis "elastic brand" limit); co-branding (Nike × Apple) and ingredient branding
  (Intel Inside, Gore-Tex).

## Core concepts to teach
The branded-house ↔ house-of-brands spectrum and the trade-offs (efficiency/clarity vs.
risk-isolation/targeting); when to sub-brand vs. endorse vs. spin out; extension as equity
leverage *and* dilution risk; how architecture decisions constrain naming (Ch 8).

## Common misconceptions
- More brands = more value (portfolio bloat destroys it — pruning is strategy).
- Extensions are "free" (each one risks the parent's associations).
- Architecture is a logo-lockup decision (it's a strategy/equity-flow decision).
