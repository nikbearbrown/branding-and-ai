## Why this chapter exists
The old book jumped to archetypes with no grounding in *what a brand is and why it has value*.
This is the foundational gap. A brand is an intangible **asset** — it changes behavior (price
premium, loyalty, preference) and shows up on balance sheets at acquisition.

## Canon & sources
- **Keller — Customer-Based Brand Equity (CBBE) pyramid:** Salience → Performance + Imagery →
  Judgments + Feelings → Resonance. Source: Kevin Lane Keller, *Strategic Brand Management*.
- **Aaker — brand equity dimensions:** brand awareness, perceived quality, brand associations,
  brand loyalty (+ proprietary assets). Source: David Aaker, *Managing Brand Equity* (1991),
  *Building Strong Brands*.
- **Brand-as-financial-asset / valuation methodologies:** Interbrand "Best Global Brands,"
  Kantar BrandZ, Brand Finance — each uses a different method (Interbrand: role-of-brand +
  brand-strength score; Brand Finance: royalty-relief). Use to show equity is *measurable money*.
  `[verify]` current league-table figures and any methodology changes before citing numbers.
- Distinguish **brand equity** (the asset) from **brand identity** (what you project) and
  **brand image** (what's perceived) — Kapferer's identity prism is useful here.

## Core concepts to teach
Awareness vs. salience; the difference between *liking* and *loyalty*; why a price premium is the
clearest equity signal; equity as the compounding return on every consistent decision (ties to the
running project's "build once, compounds" thread).

## Common misconceptions
- "Equity = awareness." Awareness is necessary, not sufficient (the resonance tiers matter).
- "Brand value = ad spend." Spend builds nothing without the equity drivers.
- Confusing a logo (identity element) with the brand (the asset in customers' heads).
