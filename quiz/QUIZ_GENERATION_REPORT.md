## Quiz Structure (Applied Uniformly)

Each quiz follows this structure:

- **Section 1 (Q1–Q5):** Bloom's Remember / Understand. Mix of three-option MCQ and True/False. Target p-value 0.77–0.85. Tests recall of named frameworks, definitions, and classifications central to the chapter.
- **Section 2 (Q6–Q9):** Bloom's Apply / Analyze. Mix of scenario-based MCQ and short-answer. Target p-value 0.50–0.70. Tests application of chapter frameworks to new scenarios and structured analysis of chapter examples.
- **Section 3 (Q10–Q11):** Bloom's Evaluate / Create. Brief-response. Target p-value 0.30–0.50. Tests synthesis and transfer — Q10 uses the chapter's own "Still Puzzling" or limits section as the evaluative frame; Q11 asks students to transfer the chapter's central logic to a new domain.

Each quiz includes:
- Learning objectives header
- Student instructions (attempt before checking)
- Full answer key with rationales for every question, including T/F
- Explanation of why each distractor is wrong (MCQ)
- Self-assessment rubric with four bands and recommended actions
- Instructor notes with Bloom's distribution, psychometric targets, common errors, and scaffolding adjustments

## Generation Rules Applied

- No generic questions — every question anchored to a chapter-specific artifact, framework, named person, or case study
- Three-option MCQ throughout (no four or five option questions)
- No negative stems (no "which is NOT")
- No absolute terms in answer options ("always," "never," "all," "none")
- No emojis except the ☐ checkbox in True/False questions
- Correct answer position randomized across A/B/C (not systematically in one slot)
- Mandatory rationale for every answer including True/False
- Bridge chapters (under 2,500 words, no anchor assignment) were not identified in chapters 14–23; all received three-section, 11-question treatment
- Chapters 20–23 are labeled as Appendix S1–S4 in file body; quiz headers reflect both designations

## Notes for Instructors

1. **Answer key access:** Each quiz file contains both the student-facing questions and the full answer key in a single document. If administering in a setting where students should not have the key, extract Q1–Q11 and suppress the Answer Key section before distribution.

2. **Bloom's weighting:** Section 3 questions are intentionally difficult (p-value 0.30–0.50). A student who scores well on Sections 1–2 but consistently misses Section 3 has surface-level recall without synthesis — the scaffolding adjustments in each quiz's Instructor Notes address this pattern.

3. **Shipit appendix chapters (Ch 20–23):** These chapters are bridge chapters in theme (applied project appendices rather than conceptual chapters) but are full-length texts with substantial original frameworks. They received the full three-section, 11-question treatment. Quiz 20 anchors to the PRD artifact; Quiz 21 anchors to the n8n pipeline and Apollo case; Quiz 22 anchors to the competitor_analyst agent specification; Quiz 23 anchors to the alignment audit and three misalignment case studies.

4. **Transfer questions (Q11):** Every quiz's Q11 applies the chapter's central logic to a domain outside brand management or AI development. These questions assess whether students have abstracted the framework (can it travel?) versus memorized its application. Common target domains include: medical device interfaces (Ch 23), research design (Ch 20), publishing economics (Ch 21), nonprofit communications (Ch 19), and immigration law (Ch 17). Grading should reward correct identification of the mechanism and its application, not surface similarity to the chapter's own examples.
